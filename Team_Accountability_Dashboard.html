3. Feature-by-Feature Walkthrough (Stranger-Friendly)

This section breaks down every view for someone who has never seen the dashboard before, explaining exactly what they are looking at, where the data comes from, and why it matters.

A. The Workspace Architecture

1. Notion-Style Resizable Sidebar

  • What: A fully resizable, collapsible sidebar matching the fluid UX of tools like Notion.
  • Why: Gives executives the power to maximize horizontal screen real estate when reviewing complex charts, while keeping navigation one click away.

2. The Centralized Inbox

  • What: An Asana-style notification hub summarizing Red Risk Alerts, Blocked Items, and Overdue Goals. Features top-level tabs (Activity, Bookmarks, Archive), granular filtering/sorting, and a dedicated Fred AI Inbox Summary to synthesize your most actionable updates.
  • Why: Eliminates the need to hunt for fires. The dashboard proactively pushes and summarizes the most critical bottlenecks directly to leadership, creating a true collaboration center.

3. Profile & OS Settings

  • What: A comprehensive control hub for Appearance (Dark Mode), Data Sync intervals, and Default landing pages.
  • Why: Personalization drives adoption. Leaders can set the dashboard to automatically refresh Notion data every 5 minutes in the background.

B. The Company Pulse (Company View)

This is the "10-second health check" for the CEO and leadership.

4. Execution Trend (Interactive Area Chart)

  • What: A visualization of output with 14/30/90-day toggles and Daily/Weekly granularity.
  • Where: Pulled from the Last Updated and Status timestamps in Notion.
  • Why: To answer, "Are we accelerating or slowing down?"

5. The Health Score & Logic Tooltips

  • What: An algorithmic score (0-100) with a hover-to-see-logic tooltip.
  • Where: Calculated in real-time based on overdue items, slippage rate, and update recency.
  • Why: To provide Transparency. Leadership can now see exactly why a score is "Amber" vs "Red".

C. The Intelligence Console (Individual View)

This is the high-density cockpit for Operations, HR, and Team Leads.

6. Velocity Sparklines (The Mini Charts)

  • What: A small bar chart next to every person's name showing their output over the last 4 weeks.
  • Where: Aggregated from tasks marked Done in Notion, grouped by week.
  • Why: To detect burnout or disengagement. If a "Top Performer" suddenly has 3 empty bars, HR knows to check in before the quarterly review.

7. The "Stale" Signal (The Clock Icon)

  • What: A visual alert for any goal that hasn't been updated in >5 days.
  • Where: Derived from the Last Edited Time property in Notion.
  • Why: "Quiet Quitting" Detection. Fast-moving teams shouldn't have dormant tasks.

D. The Squad Rollup (Squads View)

This view answers: "Which team is struggling?"

8. In-Page Squad Details Panel

  • What: Clicking on a Squad (e.g., "Engineering") opens a smooth slide-out panel outlining the squad's specific members, OKR distribution, and current blockers.
  • Why: Preserves context. You don't lose the high-level company overview when drilling down into a specific team's performance.

E. The Executive Scan (Krish Mode)

A stripped-down view for the 10-minute daily review.

9. Top 5 Risks

  • What: The 5 most critical items in the company (High Priority + Overdue/Blocked).
  • Why: To give the CEO a prioritized "hit list" for unblocking.

4. Why the Team Will Love It (The "WIIFM")

To win over the Ops and HR teams, emphasize these "Quality of Life" improvements:

  • Performance Reviews in 5 Minutes: No more chasing people for status updates. The platform provides a ready-made list of accomplishments.
  • Automated Recognition: Proactively identify top performers who are consistently hitting high velocity, ensuring they get the "Recognition" Krish mentioned.
  • Zero Double-Entry: If the team updates Notion, the dashboard is already done. There is no "extra work" for the individual contributor.
